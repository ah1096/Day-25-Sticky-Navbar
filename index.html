Welcome to the Greenhouse

a cooperative creature community

The Story

Once upon a time, in a cozy greenhouse nestled in the heart of a lush garden, there lived a small community of creatures who called it their home. Among them were a mushroom, a frog, and a mouse who shared a close friendship and looked out for one another.

One day, the peace and tranquility of the greenhouse were disturbed by the arrival of a new gardener. The human invader was determined to rid the greenhouse of its wild growth and transform it into a neat and orderly garden. At first, the creatures watched curiously as the gardener went about his work, trimming and pruning the plants with his sharp shears. But soon, they realized that the gardener's methods were far too aggressive and destructive. The mushroom, frog, and mouse decided to take action to protect their home.

The mushroom was the first to act. Using its unique ability to produce spores, it began to release a cloud of toxic particles that caused the gardener to cough and wheeze, making it difficult for him to continue his work. Next, the frog leaped into action. It hopped around the greenhouse, using its powerful legs to knock over the gardener's tools and equipment, causing him to stumble and fall. Finally, the mouse took center stage. It scurried over to the gardener's bag and began to nibble at the wires of his electric shears. Before long, the shears shorted out, rendering them useless.

With their combined efforts, the mushroom, frog, and mouse had successfully defeated the human invader. But the victory was not without its costs. The gardener lay motionless on the ground, a look of shock frozen on his face. His body was left to decay in the greenhouse, and for years after, the plants within it ran rampant over his skeleton. As time went on, the greenhouse was reclaimed by nature. The mushroom, frog, and mouse watched as the plants grew wild and free, creating a lush and vibrant landscape. They knew that their home was now safe and that they could continue to live there in peace.

And so, they lived happily ever after, in a world where nature had reclaimed what was rightfully hers, and where the balance between human progress and natural harmony had been restored.
